Redesign & creating design system

REBEL WORK

Development of brand identity system, redesign of website and web application. My contribution - UX/UI Designer, User Research, Graphical Designer

Team - 5 members, Design process - Double Diamond

During the first phase it was all about understanding the organization's brand core, persona and values. One key to do so was to facilitate workshops and iteratively develop moodboards.
The moodboards laid the ground for creating several landing pages that were tested during semi-structured interviews in order to test the feeling. Once again, workshops were key to define the navigation for the redesign of the website.
To continue, wireframes were created using Whimsical and Figma and then tested as clickable prototypes on users - iteratively.
Some of the deliverables was a new design system, logo, an outline for brand identity, icons, internal templates and a clickable prototype of the website that was scalable for phone.

Design problem

Organization was lacking a brand identity and coherent website which contributed to a not user friendly and unique design.

Design goal

Get a gathered view of the brand core and values. Go from using a black and white color palette with the goal to stand out, deepen the trust from users, have a clear purpose and of course be user friendly.


Workshop one

The goal of the first workshop was to understand and specify brand core, brand value and brand persona together with the stakeholders.
The workshop focused on understanding what the brand represents and the most important USPs.

Workshop two

During the navigation workshop, prioritizing the web site's different content and sections were in focus.
The results from the navigation workshop combined with the analyze from the user interviews, laid the ground for the design of the startpage.
Continuing phases were an iterative process of designing clickable prototypes of the wireframes, testing and redesign. Furthermore, finishing the design system and adapt the website for the phone.

PODCASTER

A UX Research project of the podcast application; Podcaster. The project at hand was to create an innovative redesign of the Podcaster application in order for it to be more social and interactive between users. My contribution - User Research, UX & UI Design.

Team - 6 member, Design process - Double Diamond

An investigation of the users main motivation was conducted on the target group; age 20-35. It included interviews combined with user statistics from the application. Thereafter, user stories could be created with different needs, behaviors and challenges. The research also included an investigation of the market.
The define phase included brainstorming, iteratively creating sketches and wireframes, and analysing the application.
Clickable prototypes were made in Sketch.
Final design delivered through a clickable prototype, a video and a documentation on how to continue the work.

Main pain points

CAN'T SHARE

No easy process to share episodes and podcasts

CAN'T SAVE EPISODES

No method for saving single episodes, only entire podcasts

NO RECOMMENDATIONS

No possibilities of seeing recommended episodes from friends

Methods

  • Wireframes
  • Personas
  • Dot-Vote
  • Think Aloud
  • Interviews
  • A/B-Testing

    Product Design

    NIMLY AB

    Development of customer registrations in CRM-system with the goal to increase sales and create better customer relations. My contribution - UX Design, Product Design, Project Management

    Team - 3 members, 4 stakeholders, Design process - Double Diamond

    User research was conducted through interviews and workshops to understand the main pain points, challenges and ambitions.
    The define phase consisted of brainstorming, defining design idea and sketching product designs.
    Clickable prototypes were made in the develop phase by developers in Python, testing the product on end users.
    Final product delivered to customer through user training.

    Main pain points

    NO ACCESS TO COMPUTER

    Salespersons are frequently visiting their customers on site and were in need of a smooth and simple way of logging their visits, and the information that was communicated.

    NO COMMON STRUCTURE

    Colleagues used different ways of registrate customer visits and information. Creating a unsatisfactory structure of information and customer contact.

    RELUCTANT TO CHANGE

    Many users were very set in their own ways of working and reluctant to change. Making it important to test the product on end users and carry out user training.

    Result

    ACCESSIBILITY

    The product worked well from the phone making the salespersons being able to add information easier from customer visits.

    USER-FRIENDLY

    User-friendly design which made the users add more customer data frequently.

    CUSTOMER RELATION

    The results from using the system made the users win more business and gain better customer relationships, and understanding the benefits of logging customer data.
